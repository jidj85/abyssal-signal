## 2026-03-28 - Spike 2: Sanity Mechanic (Session 1 of 2)

**Goal:** Test whether "burn sanity for power vs. play safe with info" creates interesting decisions.

**Done:**
- Sanity bar (100 max), displayed at bottom with threshold markers
- 3 enemies instead of 1 — makes abilities feel necessary
- Exit hex at grid edge — win condition (reach it to escape)
- Enemy telegraph system: orange diamonds show where enemies move next turn
- Eldritch Step ability (right-click, range 3, costs 20 sanity): tactical teleport
- Threshold 50 "Fractured Perception": telegraphs show real + fake positions (unreliable info)
- Threshold 25 "The Abyss Gazes Back": corrupted tiles spawn each turn (cost 5 sanity to step on), Void Pulse unlocked (Space, costs 25 sanity, kills enemies within range 2)
- Sanity = 0 means game over (mind shattered)
- R to restart after win/loss

**Open:**
- Playtest and tune: Is Eldritch Step worth 20 sanity? Is Void Pulse too expensive/cheap?
- Does the telegraph degradation actually create tension or just feel random?
- Should corrupted tiles be avoidable or is the board too small?
- Consider: passive sanity drain per turn to create urgency?
- Consider: sanity recovery mechanic (killing enemies?) to create positive loops?
- Session 2: tune based on playtest, possibly add sanity-on-kill or floor progression
